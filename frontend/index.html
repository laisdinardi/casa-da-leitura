Book of the month

If you have always been curious about Russian literature but felt it might be too heavy or distant, White Nights is the perfect place to begin. This is a short, gentle and deeply human story about loneliness, hope, and the quiet desire to be truly seen by someone.

Dostoevsky builds a narrative through nighttime encounters, delicate conversations and restrained emotions. It is not a story driven by major events, but by feelings that transcend time. The reading flows slowly, like a nighttime walk, when everything feels more intense and sincere.

This book is ideal for readers who want to explore Russian literature without fear, for those who enjoy introspective stories and for anyone who believes that some books, even short ones, stay with us long after we finish them (I swear I think about this book at least once a week since I've read it).
